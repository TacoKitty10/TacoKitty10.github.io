Tips and Hidden Gems I Wish I'd Known

While reading the Scythe trilogy, there are lots of hidden gems that I've noticed, and lots of things I wish I'd known when I was reading this series, that would've made my experience a lot more enjoyable. (Not really, this series was so good, you can't make it THAT much better)

Tips

  • When reading the books, pay attention to who wrote which journal entry. It provides valuable insight into how different scythes view gleaning and these journal entries often provide interesting insight into controversial ethical issues.

  • Always have a box of tissues nearby - for those who haven't read any of the books before, this series can be seriously emotional. (But it's still awesome, so read it!)

  • Read every word - don't skim over parts. Trust me, you'll be beating yourself up when you reach the last book - all the twists and turns were already in motion at the start. So pay attention - everything is relevant and important, even if it doesn't seem like it not, it'll all fall into place later!

  • Find a friend, or someone who you can discuss the series with. This is really important for you to have the max level of enjoyment when reading this series, because there are so many things to discuss, diss, and ship in this book, you have to have someone to fan over this series with.

Hidden Gems

  • The Ten Scythe Commandments have some similarities to the biblical commandments. E.g Scythe: 'Thou shalt kill' and Bible: 'Thou shalt not kill'

  • There's actually a lot of bible-related things in Scythe. For instance, the 'Land of Nod' in the rhyme was actually a place 'on the east of Eden', according to Genesis, the first book of the bible.
