Scythe

Scythe is the first book in the Arc of a Scythe trilogy. It introduces us to the (now perfect) world, Citra Terranova and Rowan Damisch, (The main characters) and to the problems brewing in this seemingly flawless world.

Basic Info

  • Book #: 1
  • Pg count: 443
  • Rating: 9/10

Synopsis

Scythe is set in the future where humans have been perfected so that they are now immortal. Everything is controlled by the now sentient AI known as the Thunderhead. The Thunderhead is a benevolent ruler who knows everything that has been disovered, and makes sure that everyone is safe and content. However, due to overpopulation, there are scythes. They are tasked with the task of 'gleaning' or killing, where they pick and glean people. It is a sacred duty, and there are laws for the scythes, to limit their power. Still, they are respected, and feared among society. In this first book, we meet Citra Terranova and Rowan Damisch, whom the series centers around. We follow their lives as they battle corruption and risk their own lives.

In my opinion...

In my opinion, this is one of my favourite books. It was gripping and made me laugh and cry with the characters. I will warn you, however, do not be fooled by the blurb. It sounds clich√©, but believe me - this book is entirely the opposite. Nothing is as it seems.

Thunderhead

Thunderhead is the second book in the Arc of a Scythe trilogy. In this book, Scythe Anastasia embraces her new role as a scythe, and Scythe Lucifer is now on the run for hunting and murdering corrupt scythes. Disaster is brewing as dead enemies rise from the ashes, reborn anew. Scythe Faraday travels in a race against time to find the one thing that could save them all, while the Thunderhead can only sit and watch as its precious world faces its biggest threat yet.

Basic Info

  • Book #: 2
  • Pg count: 512
  • Rating: 10/10

Synopsis

In this second book, Scythe Anastasia begins fulfilling her duties as a scythe, gleaning with compassion with her mentor, Scythe Curie alongside her. Meanwhile, Scythe Lucifer prowls among the streets, sniffing out corrupt scythes, and giving them chances at redemption. As old enemies are reborn anew, the scythes must work together to save the world, as the Thunderhead is powerless to stop the world's imminent destruction. The scythes must use the law to bring their enemies down, as the Thunderhead desperately searches for loopholes in its rules as both race against time to save the world. Scythe Faraday sets out on his quest for the founders' fail-safe, as it seems as if that is all that stands between Goddard and the world.

In my opinion...

In my opinion, though I really enjoyed Scythe, this book was even better. The twists and turns in this book - absolutely unbelievable. I stayed up way later that I should've, and often fell asleep dreaming about these new, shocking revelations, vowing to read more the next day. This book was truly spectacular, and through utter desperation, death and despair, our sycthes stay strong.

The Toll

The Toll is the final book in the Arc of a Scythe series, and in this book, scythes Anastasia, Lucifer, and Faraday dance with death in a desperate bid to save the world from Goddard. The Thunderhead is the only one who can truly save them, yet, it is forbidden to intervene in these matters. Instead, the Thunderhead must speak through the Toll, to bring all those he has exiled together, and to save the world on its behalf.

Basic Info

  • Book #: 3
  • Pg count: 640
  • Rating: 1000000/10

Synopsis

Scythes Anastasia and , Lucifer are both on the run from Goddards goons, as Goddard gains power over the world. The Thunderhead has deemed everyone unsavory, so people are helpless, as for the firts time in their life, they have had to do things for themselves. Everyone turns towards Greyson Tolliver, the only person who can communicate with the Thunderhead. Meanwhile, Scythe Faraday is desperately trying to reach the founders' failsafe. The world is on the brink of destruction, and everything rests on the shoulders of four people - Greyson, Scythe Faraday, Citra, and Rowan.

In my opinion...

In my opinion, this is my favourite book of all time. The interesting thing about this series is that it actually gets better as the series progressed. This book was filled with action, and had me sitting on the edge of my seat the whole time. When the series ended, I was absolutely shocked. Everything was wrapped up. I couldn't think of any questions. I was completely satisfied. And for the first time ever, I was happy with the last book of a series.

Citra Terranova/Scythe Anastasia

Citra Terranova is, arguably, the main character in this series. She is a brave, smart, compassionate girl, who despises scythes. She detests gleaning, but that's what makes her such a good scythe. She gleans with compassion, and respects those who she gleans.

Back to top

Rowan Damisch/Scythe Lucifer

Rowan Damisch is the second lead in the Arc of a Scythe series. He is cunning and smart, and relies on his wits and motivation to become one of the scariest scythes the world has ever seen - capable of killing other scythes if he wishes. Though Rowan seems scary, and his actions are often questionable, he remains loyal to Citra, and always has the best of intentions.

Back to top

Scythe Faraday

Scythe Faraday is Rowan and Citra's mentor. He was the one who selected them to be apprentices. Scythe Faraday is a kind, wise person, whom both Citra and Rowan love dearly. He is instrumental to both their futures.

Back to top

Scythe Curie

Scythe Curie is a wise, kind, compassionate mentor, who introduces Citra to a new side of gleaning, and how important her job actually is. She truly loves Citra, and would sacrifice her life for her. Scythe Curie is similar to Citra's grandmother - giving her wisdom and protection in the dangerous world of the scythes.

Back to top

Scythe Goddard

Scythe Goddard is the antagonist of this series. Power-hungry, conniving, and charming, Goddard uses his wits and sweet-talking skills to win scythes over. He promotes gleaning as a sport, and completely disregards the old-guard ways. He will be the one to lead the world to destruction, but hey, at least he's the leader, right? The scariest thing about Goddard is the fact that he actually believes what he teaches - he's lied so much, even he believes in himself. Totally deranged.

Back to top

The Thunderhead

The Thunderhead is the benevolent ruler of the world. It was originally known as 'the cloud' until it become a sentient AI being, and resumed control of the world. However, the Thunderhead never has, and never will control humans, as he treasures them, similar to a parent. It simply protects them from harm, and ensures that they are happy and content. The Thunderhead, however, is forbidden from interfering in scythe affairs. But as it is all-seeing and all-knowing, it doesn't miss the growing tension between the scythes, and can accurately predict whatever misfortunes that are about to occur. It must work around the rules to protect its beloved humanity from total destruction.

Back to top

Greyson Tolliver/The Toll

Greyson is a devoted follower of the Thunderhead. His dream was to become a Nimbus agent and serve the Thunderhead. But why, you ask. Well, the reason for this devotion, or, as some might call it, love, is due to the fact that the Thunderhead is like a parent to Greyson. His biological parents were always away, and they never really cared about their kids, so the Thunderhead was the one who always asked Greyson how his day was, and so on. Little did Greyson know, this bond between him and the Thunderhead was one of the most important things in the future, and he would save many lives. Greyson is a kind, smart, and simple person, and he saves many of our beloved scythes.

Back to top

Scythe Rand

Scythe Rand is a devoted follower of Goddard's. She's quite psychopathic, and extremely cunning and skilled. However, she does have a weakness - Tyger Salazar. Rand lives life for herself, and if anyone tries to stop her, they'll find themselves on the wrond edge of a sword.

Back to top

Jerico Soberanis

Jerico, or more commonly known as Jeri, is a Madagascan. When the sun is out, Jeri is a woman, but when the clouds cover the sun, Jeri is a man. Jeri's an accomplished sailor, and a beloved captain. Smart, quick-witted, strong and self-sacrificing, Jeri is one of the key characters of this series, but only comes in the later books.
